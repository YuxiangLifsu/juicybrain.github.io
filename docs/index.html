My research has been extended to functional genomics thereafter at the Chinese University of Hong Kong. Since April 2019, I have been doing research as a postdoctoral scholar at Florida State University on epigenetic mechanisms underpinning drug addiction. SfN 2023

Epigenetic regulation of drug addiction

Illicit drug abuse burdens all societies in multiple ways, including impacts on human health, productivity, and social security. Intriguingly, only about 10% to 16% of individuals become addicted to drugs. Although we know that environmental, genetic, and epigenetic risk factors contribute to the etiology, the underlying molecular mechanisms remain elusive due to the complex anatomical characterization of the human brain. Utilizing mouse behavior models and low-cell number NGS sequencing techniques such as whole-genome bisulfite sequencing, ATAC-seq, Hi-C, and RNA-seq, along with bioinformatic expertise, I am investigating drug addiction-related epigenetic alterations in cell type-specific neurons within brain regions associated with drug addiction.

Transcription factor Atlas of Nicotina benthamiana

Cistrome profiling in plants

The gene expression of organisms is the result of the well-coordinated interplay of numerous biomacromolecules including the genome and other transcription factors or transcription regulators. With the advancement of massively parallel sequencing technologies, various tools have been developed for a deeper understanding of the fundamental mechanisms of gene transcription and regulation. Combining chromatin immunoprecipitation sequencing(ChIP-seq) by Tn5 tagmentation and Agrobacterium-mediated transient expression, I have developed a fast and easy-to-apply method and profiled the whole-genome binding sites of more than 100 transcription factors in tobacco (Nicotiana benthamiana).

DNA damage-binding protein 1 (DDB1)

Epigenetic regulation in fruit development

DNA damage-binding protein 1 (DDB1) is a component of the UV-DDB complex and CUL4-DDB1 E3 ligase complex, playing essential biological roles across kingdoms of fungi, plants and animals. Point mutations or other forms of impairment of DDB1 leads to genetic disorders in humans and drastic accumulation of pigments in tomato. We find that DDB1 physically interact with multiple epigenetic factors including Methyl-DNA binding protein(MBD5), and heterochromatin protein 1 (HP1) in tomato. With biochemical and genetic evidence, our studies indicate that multi-facets of epigenetic pathways are involved in the regulatory roles of DDB1 in fruit development.
