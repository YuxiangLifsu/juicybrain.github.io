Hi, I am Yuxiang. I am from Sichuan, P.R. China. I received my PhD degree at Sichuan University in Chengdu city working on epigenetic regulation of fruit development using tomato as a model. My research has been extended to functional genomics thereafter at the Chinese University of Hong Kong, where I developed an assay combining ChIP-seq by Tn5 tagmentation and Agrobacterium-mediated transient expression to profile the transcription factor (TF)-binding events in plants. Using this technique, I performed ChIP-seq of more than 100 TFs in Nicotiana benthamiana (a type of tobacco) leaves. Since April 2019, I have been working as a postdoctoral scholar at Florida State University on epigenetic mechanisms underpinning drug addiction using mouse models. Here, in collaboration with other researchers, I investigate the drug (cocaine, alcohol, heroin) addiction -associated DNA methylation and chromatin structure changes in mouse brains. When I am out of the lab, I usually play basketball, football/soccer, skateboarding, badminton or tennis, or spend my time in the woods.
